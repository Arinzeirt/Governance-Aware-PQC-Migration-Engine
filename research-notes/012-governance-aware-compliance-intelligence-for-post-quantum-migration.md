# Research Notes Series 012

# Governance-Aware Compliance Intelligence for Post-Quantum Migration

---

## Building on Research Note 011

Research Note 011 proposed that **Business Criticality** should become the primary driver of enterprise post-quantum migration rather than cryptographic severity alone.

The research demonstrated that organizations ultimately migrate business capabilities, not algorithms, and that governance decisions should prioritize systems according to operational importance, customer impact and business resilience.

However, another governance challenge remains.

Even when business priorities are clearly established, organizations must still satisfy an increasingly complex regulatory landscape.

This raises an important question:

> **How should regulatory obligations influence post-quantum migration decisions?**

---

# Research Observation

Many enterprise security programmes continue to treat compliance as a documentation exercise that occurs after technical implementation.

In most post-quantum migration initiatives, regulatory frameworks are used primarily for:

- Audit preparation
- Policy documentation
- Evidence collection
- Compliance reporting

While these activities remain important, they rarely influence the migration decisions themselves.

As a result, organizations risk implementing technically sound migration plans that overlook regulatory priorities and governance expectations.

---

# Research Problem

Current post-quantum migration approaches rarely integrate regulatory intelligence directly into migration planning.

Without governance-aware compliance analysis, organizations may:

- Delay migration of regulated systems.
- Underestimate regulatory exposure.
- Prioritize technically vulnerable assets while overlooking compliance obligations.
- Increase audit complexity.
- Create inconsistent migration priorities across business units.

This disconnect prevents compliance from serving as a strategic decision-support capability.

---

# Proposed Contribution

This research introduces the concept of **Governance-Aware Compliance Intelligence (GACI)** for enterprise post-quantum migration.

Rather than treating compliance as a passive reporting requirement, regulatory obligations become active decision variables within migration planning.

Compliance intelligence evaluates factors such as:

- DORA operational resilience requirements
- PCI DSS cryptographic protection expectations
- NIST post-quantum migration guidance
- ISO/IEC 27001 governance controls
- Internal enterprise security policies
- Regulatory deadlines
- Evidence requirements
- Governance accountability

These factors directly influence migration priority, scheduling and executive decision making.

---

# Practical Validation Through EQMP

The proposed compliance intelligence model is currently being implemented within the **Enterprise Quantum Migration Platform (EQMP).**

EQMP extends traditional cryptographic assessment by evaluating regulatory exposure through governance-aware indicators including:

- DORA impact
- PCI DSS impact
- NIST alignment
- Compliance risk level
- Compliance recommendations
- Governance ownership
- Recommended migration deadlines
- Executive reporting

Instead of producing compliance reports after migration planning, the platform incorporates regulatory intelligence into migration decisions from the beginning of the assessment process.

This implementation provides an experimental environment for validating governance-aware compliance intelligence within enterprise post-quantum migration.

---

# Research Contribution

The primary contribution of this research is the integration of compliance intelligence into enterprise migration governance.

Rather than separating technical assessment from regulatory obligations, this work proposes a unified governance model where compliance actively shapes migration priorities, implementation sequencing and executive oversight.

This transforms compliance from an audit function into a strategic component of enterprise post-quantum decision making.

---

# Key Insight

> **Compliance should not merely validate migration decisions—it should help determine them.**
