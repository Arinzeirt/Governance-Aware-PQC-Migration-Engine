# Research Notes Series 015

# Introducing the Enterprise Quantum Migration Platform (EQMP): A Governance-Driven Architecture for Enterprise Post-Quantum Readiness

---

## Building on Research Notes 001–014

The previous fourteen research notes progressively developed the conceptual foundations of governance-aware post-quantum migration.

The research evolved through several stages:

- Enterprise cryptographic discovery
- Asset classification
- Coverage assessment
- Governance-aware migration
- Decision support
- Runtime orchestration
- Explainable migration intelligence
- Enterprise readiness assessment
- Governance-driven migration roadmaps
- Business criticality analysis
- Compliance intelligence
- Executive decision intelligence
- Integrated platform architecture

Collectively, these contributions demonstrate that enterprise post-quantum migration extends far beyond replacing vulnerable cryptographic algorithms.

Instead, successful migration requires continuous governance, business alignment, regulatory awareness and executive decision support.

This research culminates in the introduction of a unified enterprise platform capable of integrating these capabilities into a single operational environment.

---

# Research Observation

Most enterprise post-quantum initiatives remain fragmented.

Organizations frequently deploy separate solutions for:

- Cryptographic discovery
- Certificate inventory
- Risk assessment
- Compliance management
- Governance reporting
- Executive dashboards

Although individually valuable, these tools rarely operate as an integrated migration ecosystem.

This fragmentation increases operational complexity, reduces governance visibility and slows enterprise migration programmes.

---

# Research Problem

Current enterprise migration approaches generally lack an integrated governance architecture capable of supporting the complete migration lifecycle.

As a consequence, organizations often experience:

- Disconnected migration planning
- Inconsistent governance decisions
- Fragmented compliance evidence
- Poor executive visibility
- Limited migration orchestration
- Difficulty measuring enterprise readiness

Without an integrated platform, migration becomes a collection of isolated technical projects rather than a coordinated enterprise transformation programme.

---

# Proposed Contribution

This research introduces the **Enterprise Quantum Migration Platform (EQMP)**.

EQMP is proposed as a governance-driven enterprise platform that transforms technical cryptographic evidence into business intelligence, regulatory insight and executive decision support.

Rather than functioning solely as a cryptographic scanner, EQMP integrates multiple governance capabilities into a unified architecture.

The proposed platform consists of:

- Discovery Engine
- Classification Engine
- Coverage Assessment Engine
- Governance Intelligence Engine
- Readiness Assessment Engine
- Compliance Intelligence Engine
- Executive Decision Intelligence Engine
- Migration Roadmap Engine
- Reporting Engine
- Runtime Orchestration Engine
- Enterprise Command Center

Each capability contributes structured intelligence that supports enterprise-wide post-quantum migration.

---

# Practical Validation Through EQMP

The concepts presented throughout this research series are currently being implemented within the Enterprise Quantum Migration Platform.

The current prototype demonstrates governance-aware capabilities including:

- Automated cryptographic discovery
- Asset classification
- Coverage assessment
- Enterprise readiness scoring
- Business criticality evaluation
- Governance ownership assignment
- Regulatory impact analysis
- Compliance recommendations
- Executive reporting
- Migration prioritization
- Runtime orchestration
- Interactive executive dashboards

Unlike conventional cryptographic inventory tools, EQMP is designed to support the complete governance lifecycle of enterprise post-quantum migration.

The platform serves as an experimental environment for validating governance-aware migration methodologies in real-world enterprise scenarios.

---

# Research Contribution

The principal contribution of this work is the proposal of a governance-driven enterprise architecture for post-quantum migration.

Rather than viewing migration as a cryptographic replacement exercise, EQMP positions migration as a continuous governance capability integrating technical assessment, business priorities, regulatory intelligence and executive decision support.

This research therefore extends enterprise post-quantum migration beyond technology implementation toward organizational transformation.
