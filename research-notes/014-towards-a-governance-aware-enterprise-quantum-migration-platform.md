# Research Notes Series 014

# Towards a Governance-Aware Enterprise Quantum Migration Platform

---

## Building on Research Note 013

Research Note 013 introduced the concept of an **Executive Decision Intelligence Engine (EDIE)**, demonstrating how technical evidence, governance intelligence, compliance obligations and business priorities can be transformed into executive-ready recommendations.

This capability addressed one of the largest gaps in enterprise post-quantum migration by enabling leadership to make informed decisions without requiring deep cryptographic expertise.

However, another question naturally follows.

If discovery, governance, readiness assessment, compliance intelligence and executive decision support are all essential capabilities, **how should they operate together within a single enterprise platform?**

This question motivates the next stage of the research.

---

# Research Observation

Most existing post-quantum migration solutions focus on individual capabilities.

Some products specialize in:

- Cryptographic discovery
- Algorithm inventory
- Certificate management
- Vulnerability reporting
- Compliance assessment

These capabilities are valuable, but they often exist as disconnected tools requiring significant manual coordination.

As organizations grow, fragmented assessment workflows increase operational complexity and reduce governance visibility.

Enterprise migration therefore requires an integrated architectural approach rather than isolated security functions.

---

# Research Problem

Current enterprise migration programmes frequently rely on multiple independent tools to support discovery, assessment, governance and reporting.

This fragmentation creates several challenges:

- Duplicate assessment activities.
- Inconsistent migration priorities.
- Disconnected governance workflows.
- Limited executive visibility.
- Difficult regulatory reporting.
- Poor coordination between technical and business stakeholders.

Without an integrated platform architecture, post-quantum migration becomes increasingly difficult to manage at enterprise scale.

---

# Proposed Contribution

This research proposes the **Enterprise Quantum Migration Platform (EQMP)** as a governance-aware enterprise architecture for post-quantum migration.

Rather than functioning as a traditional cryptographic scanner, EQMP integrates multiple governance capabilities into a unified decision-support platform.

The proposed architecture consists of several interconnected components:

- Discovery Engine
- Classification Engine
- Coverage Assessment Engine
- Governance Intelligence Engine
- Readiness Assessment Engine
- Compliance Intelligence Engine
- Executive Decision Intelligence Engine
- Migration Roadmap Engine
- Reporting Engine
- Enterprise Dashboard
- Runtime Orchestration Engine

Each component contributes structured intelligence that supports governance-driven migration planning across the enterprise.

---

# Practical Validation Through EQMP

The proposed architecture is currently being implemented as a working prototype through the **Enterprise Quantum Migration Platform (EQMP).**

The platform presently supports capabilities including:

- Enterprise cryptographic discovery
- Asset classification
- Coverage assessment
- Readiness scoring
- Business criticality analysis
- Governance ownership
- Regulatory impact assessment
- Compliance recommendations
- Executive reporting
- Migration prioritization
- Runtime assessment workflow
- Interactive executive dashboard

Together, these components demonstrate how governance-aware migration intelligence can be operationalized within a unified enterprise platform.

The ongoing implementation serves as an experimental environment for evaluating integrated governance-driven post-quantum migration.

---

# Research Contribution

The contribution of this work is the introduction of an integrated enterprise architecture that unifies technical assessment, governance intelligence and executive decision support.

Rather than viewing post-quantum migration as a sequence of isolated technical activities, this research proposes an architectural model in which governance becomes the coordinating layer across discovery, assessment, planning and migration execution.

This establishes a foundation for enterprise-scale post-quantum transformation.

---

# Key Insight

> **Enterprise post-quantum migration is not a collection of security tools—it is an integrated governance platform that transforms technical evidence into enterprise action.**
