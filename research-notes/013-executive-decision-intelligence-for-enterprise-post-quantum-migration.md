# Research Notes Series 013

# Executive Decision Intelligence for Enterprise Post-Quantum Migration

---

## Building on Research Note 012

Research Note 012 introduced **Governance-Aware Compliance Intelligence (GACI)**, demonstrating that regulatory obligations should actively influence post-quantum migration decisions rather than simply validate completed implementations.

By integrating compliance into governance, migration planning becomes aligned with both business priorities and regulatory expectations.

However, another challenge remains.

Enterprise leaders rarely make migration decisions based on cryptographic algorithms, technical reports or compliance matrices.

Instead, executives require clear answers to questions such as:

- What is our current risk?
- Which business services should migrate first?
- What is the financial impact?
- Are we meeting regulatory expectations?
- What decision should we make next?

This introduces the next stage of the research.

---

# Research Observation

Current post-quantum migration tools primarily generate technical outputs including:

- Cryptographic inventories
- Vulnerability findings
- Algorithm identification
- Technical recommendations
- Compliance reports

Although these outputs are valuable for engineering teams, they rarely provide executives with actionable decision intelligence.

Executive leadership requires governance-focused information rather than technical evidence.

Without executive decision support, organizations struggle to convert technical findings into strategic action.

---

# Research Problem

Most existing post-quantum migration solutions stop after producing assessment reports.

Consequently, executives are often required to interpret highly technical information before making governance decisions.

This creates several challenges:

- Technical findings are difficult for executive leadership to interpret.
- Business priorities become disconnected from engineering assessments.
- Migration investments become difficult to justify.
- Governance committees lack measurable decision support.
- Executive reporting remains fragmented across technical and compliance teams.

The absence of executive intelligence slows enterprise migration.

---

# Proposed Contribution

This research introduces the concept of an **Executive Decision Intelligence Engine (EDIE)** for enterprise post-quantum migration.

Rather than presenting technical findings directly to leadership, the engine transforms governance evidence into executive-ready recommendations.

The proposed model combines:

- Enterprise readiness
- Business criticality
- Compliance intelligence
- Governance ownership
- Operational resilience
- Migration priority
- Regulatory exposure
- Executive risk indicators

These dimensions are synthesized into actionable governance recommendations that support executive decision making.

---

# Practical Validation Through EQMP

The proposed Executive Decision Intelligence Engine is currently being implemented within the **Enterprise Quantum Migration Platform (EQMP).**

EQMP already integrates multiple governance-aware capabilities, including:

- Executive readiness scoring
- Business criticality assessment
- Governance ownership analysis
- Compliance intelligence
- Migration prioritization
- Recommended migration deadlines
- Executive reporting
- Governance recommendations

Instead of generating technical reports alone, the platform delivers executive-focused insights designed to support board-level discussions, investment planning and enterprise migration governance.

This implementation provides an experimental environment for validating executive decision intelligence within post-quantum migration programmes.

---

# Research Contribution

The contribution of this work is the introduction of a governance-driven decision support capability specifically designed for executive leadership.

Rather than expecting executives to interpret cryptographic evidence, the proposed framework transforms technical, business and regulatory intelligence into strategic recommendations.

This shifts enterprise migration from technical reporting toward informed executive governance.

Instead of asking:

> "Which algorithms are vulnerable?"

Executive leadership can ask:

> **"What decision should we make today to improve enterprise post-quantum readiness?"**

---

# Key Insight

> **Executives do not need cryptographic findings—they need governance intelligence that enables confident decisions.**
